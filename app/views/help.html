Introducing the ePER

What is the ePER?

The ePER is a part-digital version of the Person Escort Record. It is currently being piloted at a number of prisons in England.

How is it different from the paper PER?

You complete the ePER on our online service, which:

  • uses information from NOMIS to automatically complete some details
  • remembers and reuses answers from previous PERs
  • highlights important risk and healthcare information for escort staff

What should it be used for?

The ePER should be used for all court journeys, inter-prison transfers and hospital journeys from your establishment.

Is it all done online?

Not yet. The ePER replaces the first two pages of the paper PER (front cover and risk indicator).

You'll complete the following sections on our online service:

  • Prisoner details
  • Move details
  • Offences
  • Risk
  • Healthcare

Once complete, you can print off the ePER and staple it to the 'Escort handover details' booklet.

How to register to use the ePER system

See all steps
  1. Open your account invitation email.
  2. Click ‘Setup my account’.
  3. Enter your name and password, and click ‘Setup my account’.
  4. You’ll be directed to the ePER service. Click ‘Start now’.
  5. Sign in with your email address and password.
  6. You’re now signed in to the ePER service.
  7. Click the start icon in your browser bar to bookmark the page. This will add the service to your browser’s bookmarks folder.

How to create an ePER

See all steps
  1. Enter a prisoner number and search.
  2. Click ‘Start new PER’.
  3. Check or complete the detainee information.
  4. Save your answers.
  5. Complete the move information.
  6. Save your answers.
  7. Scoll to the Offences section and click ‘Edit’.
  8. Check or complete the offences information. You can add more offences if you need to.
  9. Save your answers.
  10. The ePER is ready for Risk and Healthcare information to be added.
  11. Click ‘Return to home’ to go back to the dashboard.
  12. The new ePER has been added to the dashboard.

How to complete the risk section of the ePER

See all steps
  1. Click on the prisoner number.
  2. Scroll to the Risk section and click ‘Edit’.
  3. Answer the questions. Check NOMIS and intelligence sources for risk information.
  4. Click ‘Save and continue’ to see the next screen.
  5. On the summary screen, check your answers.
  6. Click ‘Change’ to go back to a question.
  7. Click ‘Save and view summary’ to go back to the summary screen.
  8. Click ‘Confirm and save’ to save your answers.
  9. The Risk section is now complete.
  10. Click ‘Return to home’ to go back to the dashboard.
  11. The dashboard shows that the Risk section is complete.

Complete the health section

See all steps
  1. Click on the prisoner number.
  2. Scroll to the Healthcare section and click ‘Edit’.
  3. Answer the questions.
  4. Click ‘Save and continue’ to go to the next screen.
  5. On the summary screen, check your answers.
  6. Click ‘Change’ to go back to a question.
  7. Click ‘Save and view summary’ to go back to the summary screen.
  8. Click ‘Confirm and save’ to save your answers.
  9. The Healthcare section is now complete.
  10. Click ‘Return to home’ to go back to the dashboard.
  11. The dashboard shows that the Healthcare section is complete.

How to print an ePER

See all steps
  1. Make sure the Risk, Health and Offences sections are complete.
  2. Click ‘Print’. This opens a preview of the ePER in a new window.
  3. Click the print icon in the top right-hand corner of the screen. Click ‘Print’.
  4. On the dashboard, the ePER status will change to ‘Issued’ when it has been printed.

How to reprint a PER

See all steps
  1. If an ePER is ‘Issued’, it has already been printed.
  2. Click on the prisoner number.
  3. Scroll to the bottom of the ePER.
  4. Click ‘Reprint’
  5. Click ‘Reprint this PER’. This opens a preview of the ePER in a new window.
  6. Click the print icon in the top right-hand corner of the screen.
  7. Click ‘Print’

How to cancel a PER

See all steps
  1. Click on the prisoner number.
  2. Scroll to the bottom of the ePER
  3. Click ‘Cancel PER’
  4. Enter the reason for cancelling the PER
  5. Click ‘Cancel this PER’
  6. The PER is cancelled. The reason for the cancellation will be displayed on the dashboard.

How to manage user accounts

See all steps

Adding a user to your team

  1. Go to www.signon.dsd.io/users/sign_in and sign in using your email address and password.
  2. Scroll to ‘Your teams’ and click ‘Moving People Safely’.
  3. You’ll see users who already have access to the service.
  4. Click ‘Add user’.
  5. Enter their email address.
  6. If you want them to be an admin user, tick ‘Is admin?’.
  7. Click ‘Add user’.
  8. The new user is added to the team. They will be sent an email invite to accept.

Removing admin rights

  1. Scroll to ‘Your teams’ and click ‘Moving People Safely’.
  2. Find the user.
  3. Click ‘Remove admin rights’.
  4. The user’s admin rights have been removed.
  5. To restore their admin rights, click ‘Make admin’.

Removing a user from your team

  1. Scroll to ‘Your teams’ and click ‘Moving People Safely’.
  2. Find the user.
  3. Click ‘Remove from team’.
  4. The user is removed from the team.
